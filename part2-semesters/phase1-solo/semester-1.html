From Cell to Score

Semester 1: Solo Instrument — Gestural Vocabulary

"Timbre first! The inner beauty of the colour of sound."

Overview

Semester 1 focuses on building a gestural vocabulary for solo instrument. While Berklee's syllabus addresses "solo instrument excluding piano," we are taking the unusual and rewarding path of exploring the tenor recorder—an instrument with deep Renaissance and Baroque roots but significant contemporary repertoire.

The core question: How does a solo piece hold together without accompaniment?

Key Concepts This Semester

  • Gesture as the organizing principle of solo wind writing
  • Breath as structural punctuation
  • Registral stratification creating implied polyphony
  • Motivic economy — maximum expression from minimal material
  • Timbre-first approach to sound and technique

Timeline

Given advanced preparation, the standard syllabus requirements may be satisfied in weeks 1-4, with remaining weeks devoted to extended exploration (extended techniques, additional forms, or early chamber preparation).
